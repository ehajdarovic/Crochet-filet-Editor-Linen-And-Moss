The Binary Fabric

"Before the silicon chip, there was the spool of thread. In filet crochet, we compiled patterns stitch by stitch—inventing loops and spaces as the original 1s and 0s."

As a creative technologist, I see computation not just as a tool for screens, but as a continuation of age-old craft techniques. The Jacquard loom and punch-card memory systems are direct ancestors of modern computers, but the conceptual core goes even deeper into handcrafts.

In Filet Crochet, the artist operates as a manual parser. Every block is composed of a rigid set of instructions (usually double crochets to form a solid "block", or double crochets separated by chains to form an open "space"). When these cells are aligned on a rectangular coordinate system, they compose complex raster images out of tension and space.

This project exposes the logic of the hand. By taking the physical rearing horse artwork—built block-by-block with fine thread—and converting it back into a digital mesh, we complete a circle of representation. This editor acts as a compilation environment, translating physical pixels into digital code and back, letting users draw, manipulate thresholds, and export new templates for physical realization.
