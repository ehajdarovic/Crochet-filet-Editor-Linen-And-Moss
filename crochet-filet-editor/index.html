The Ecology of Fiber

Crochet filet is a binary language written in tension and space. In this environment, we treat the canvas not as a cold raster grid, but as a living linen membrane where individual crochet stitches behave like mycelial threads or lichen structures growing on organic soil. Warp and weft threads sway with ambient wind currents, and connections emerge dynamically where cells interlink.
